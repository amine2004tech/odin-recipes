Moroccan hrira

  • Olive Oil: 2 tablespoons
  • Celery: 2 stalks, finely chopped
  • Onion: 1 large, chopped
  • Fresh Cilantro: 1/2 cup, chopped
  • Fresh Parsley: 1/2 cup, chopped
  • Tomatoes: 4 large, peeled and chopped (or 1 can of diced tomatoes)
  • Tomato Paste: 2 tablespoons
  • Coriander: 1 teaspoon
  • Paprika: 1 teaspoon
  • Flour: 1/4 cup (for thickening)
  • Salt: 1 teaspoon
  • Black Pepper: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Chicken Broth: 1 cup
  • Lemon: 1, cut into wedges (for serving)

  1. Saut√© Vegetables:

    In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, celery, and carrot. Cook until the vegetables are softened, about 5-7 minutes.

  2. Add Aromatics:

    Stir in the minced garlic, cilantro, and parsley. Cook for another 2 minutes until fragrant.

  3. Add the chopped tomatoes and tomato paste to the pot:

    Stir well. Add the cinnamon stick, ginger, turmeric, paprika, cumin, black pepper, and salt. Cook for 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.

  4. Stir in the lentils and chickpeas:

    Pour in the chicken broth.Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the lentils are tender and the soup has thickened slightly.

  5. Thicken the Soup:

    In a small bowl, mix the flour with the water to create a slurry. Gradually stir this mixture into the soup. Continue to simmer for another 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the soup reaches your desired thickness.

  6. Adjust Seasoning:

    taste the soup and adjust seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed.

  7. Serve:

    Remove the cinnamon stick. Ladle the soup into bowls. Serve with lemon wedges on the side for squeezing over the soup.
