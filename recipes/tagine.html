Moroccan tagine

  • Chicken: 4 chicken thighs (bone-in, skinless) or 2 whole chicken legs
  • Olive Oil: 2 tablespoons
  • Onion: 1 large, chopped
  • Garlic: 3 cloves, minced
  • Ginger: 1 tablespoon, minced
  • Cinnamon: 1 stick
  • Cumin: 1 teaspoon
  • Coriander: 1 teaspoon
  • Paprika: 1 teaspoon
  • Turmeric: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Salt: 1 teaspoon
  • Black Pepper: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Chicken Broth: 1 cup
  • Lemon: 1, cut into wedges (for serving)

  1. Prepare the Chicken:

    Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or tagine over medium heat. Add the chicken pieces and brown them on all sides. Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.

  2. Sauté Aromatics:

    In the same skillet, add the chopped onion and cook until it’s soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Add the minced garlic and ginger and cook for another minute.

  3. Add Spices:

    Stir in the cinnamon stick, cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, salt, and black pepper. Cook for 1-2 minutes to toast the spices and release their flavors.

  4. Combine Ingredients:

    Return the browned chicken to the skillet. Add the chicken broth and chopped apricots. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer.

  5. Simmer:

    Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the chicken is cooked through and tender. The sauce should thicken slightly.

  6. Add Almonds (Optional)

    If using almonds, stir them in during the last 10 minutes of cooking.

  7. Garnish and Serve:

    Garnish with fresh chopped cilantro. Serve the tagine with lemon wedges on the side, and enjoy with couscous or crusty bread.

ENJOY!
